LEE KUAN YEW

SINGAPORE'S FOUNDING FATHER

L.K.Y giving a speech.

Mr Lee Kuan Yew was the first Prime Minister of Singapore, governing for three decades. Lee is recognised as the nation's founding father, with the country described as transitioning from the "third world to first world in a single generation" under his leadership.

The following timeline describes his life briefly.

  • 1923 - Born in Singapore
  • 1935 - Topped in island exam in Telok Kurau English School.
  • 1940 - Came in first in Senior Cambridge examinations at Raffles Institution in Singapore.
  • 1942 - Survived World War II during the infamous Japanese Occupation.
  • 1946 - Set Sail to England, Enrolled in London School of Economics.
  • 1947 - Moved to Cambridge University & tied the knot in secret with Ms Kwa Geok Choo.
  • 1949 - Graduated in Law with First Class Honours.
  • 1950 - Officially married after returning to Singapore.
  • 1952 - Began political activism and became legal advisor to more than 100 different unions and associations within a span of 2 years.
  • 1954 - Launched People's Action Party (PAP) at Victoria Memorial Hall
  • 1959 - PAP won 43 out of 51 seats. Mr Lee became Singapore’s first Prime Minister.
  • 1960 - Enacted a law to wipe out corruption and establish a clean and effective government.
  • 1961 - Set up the Economic Development Board to draw foreign investments to an industrialising Singapore.
  • 1963 - Malaysia was formed, comprising Malaya, Singapore, Sabah and Sarawak.
  • 1963 - Launched a home ownership scheme which enabled 91 per cent of Housing Board residents to own their homes by 1996.
  • 1965 - Singapore separated from Malaysia on Aug 9. Mr Lee wept on national TV, saying he had believed his whole life in the unity of the two territories.
  • 1967 - Full-time national service was introduced to build Singapore’s defence force ahead of British troops’ pullout in 1971.
  • 1990 - Stepped down as Prime Minster after 31 years and succeeded by Mr Goh Chok Tong. Appointed Senior Minister on Nov 28.
  • 1998 - Published first volume of memoirs The Singapore Story. Subsquent;y, the second volume, From Third World To First: The Singapore Story, was launched in 2000.
  • 2004 - Assumed the post of Minister Mentor after his son Hsien Loong became PM, and Mr Goh was made Senior Minister.
  • 2010 - Mrs Lee died on Oct 2 after a long illness caused by strokes.
  • 2011 - Stepped down from the Cabinet.
  • 2015 - Mr Lee Kuan Yew dies aged 91 at 3.18am on March 23.
